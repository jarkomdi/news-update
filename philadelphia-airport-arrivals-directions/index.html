Philadelphia Airport Arrivals Directions

Philadelphia International Airport is located approximately 7 miles southwest of downtown Philadelphia. The airport is easily accessible by car, taxi, or public transportation. Here are the different options for getting to the airport:

By Car:

If you're driving to the airport, you can take Interstate 95 or Interstate 76 to reach your destination. The airport is well-connected to the major highways in the area, making it convenient for travelers coming from different parts of the city or even from neighboring states. There are several parking options available at the airport, including short-term and long-term parking facilities.

By Taxi:

If you prefer to take a taxi, you can easily find one outside the airport terminals. Taxis are a convenient option for travelers who don't want to deal with the hassle of driving or parking. Make sure to use a reputable taxi service to ensure a safe and comfortable journey.

By Public Transportation:

Philadelphia International Airport is well-connected to the city's public transportation network. SEPTA, the local transit authority, operates several bus and train routes that serve the airport. The Airport Line train, also known as the SEPTA Regional Rail, is a popular choice for travelers. The train departs from the airport terminals and takes passengers directly to downtown Philadelphia and other major destinations in the city.
