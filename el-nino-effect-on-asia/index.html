El Nino Effect on Asia: Exploring the Phenomenon

El Nino is a climate pattern characterized by warm ocean currents in the Pacific Ocean. This phenomenon occurs when there is a weakening of the trade winds, resulting in a warming of the surface waters. The warm ocean currents then influence global weather patterns, leading to changes in temperature and precipitation. In Asia, El Nino can result in prolonged droughts, heatwaves, and reduced monsoon rainfall, causing water scarcity and affecting agricultural productivity.
