Koh Samui Airport to Bangrak Pier: The Journey

Getting from Koh Samui Airport to Bangrak Pier is a relatively straightforward journey. There are several transportation options available, including taxis, private transfers, and shared minivans. The journey takes approximately 15-20 minutes, depending on traffic conditions.

To begin your journey, you can either pre-book a transfer service or arrange transportation upon arrival at the airport. Taxis are readily available outside the terminal, and you can also find minivan services offering shared transfers. Private transfers are a convenient option if you prefer a more personalized and hassle-free experience.

Once you've reached Bangrak Pier, you'll have access to various ferry services that can take you to other destinations in the Gulf of Thailand. The pier is a popular departure point for travelers heading to Koh Phangan, with frequent ferry departures throughout the day. Make sure to check the ferry schedules in advance to plan your journey accordingly.

About Koh Samui Airport and Bangrak Pier

Koh Samui Airport, also known as Samui International Airport, is the main gateway to the popular Thai island of Koh Samui. It serves both domestic and international flights, with direct connections to major cities in Southeast Asia. The airport is located in the northeastern part of the island, making it easily accessible from various accommodations and tourist areas.

Bangrak Pier, also known as Big Buddha Pier, is one of the main piers on Koh Samui. It is located in the northeast of the island, near the famous Big Buddha Statue. The pier is a hub for ferry services to neighboring islands, including Koh Phangan and Koh Tao. It offers a convenient and scenic way to explore the stunning islands of the Gulf of Thailand.
