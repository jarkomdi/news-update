Tips for Making Philadelphia Cream Cheese Cheesecake Ingredients

When making a Philadelphia cream cheese cheesecake, there are a few tips to keep in mind. First, be sure to let the cream cheese come to room temperature before using it. This will ensure that it mixes smoothly and evenly with the other ingredients. Also, be sure to mix the ingredients until they are just combined. Overmixing can lead to a dense and heavy cheesecake.

Another tip is to use a water bath when baking the cheesecake. This helps to prevent cracking and ensures even baking. Simply place the cheesecake pan in a larger pan filled with hot water before baking. The steam created from the water bath will help to keep the cheesecake moist and prevent it from drying out.

How to Make Philadelphia Cream Cheese Cheesecake Ingredients

Now that you know all about Philadelphia cream cheese cheesecake ingredients, it's time to learn how to make it! Here's a simple recipe to get you started:

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups graham cracker crumbs
  • 1/2 cup melted butter
  • 3 8oz packages of Philadelphia cream cheese, softened
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 3 eggs
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract

Instructions:

  1. Preheat your oven to 325°F (163°C).
  2. In a bowl, mix the graham cracker crumbs and melted butter until well combined. Press the mixture into the bottom of a 9-inch springform pan to form the crust.
  3. In a separate bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and sugar until smooth and creamy.
  4. Add the eggs, one at a time, beating well after each addition. Stir in the vanilla extract.
  5. Pour the cream cheese mixture over the crust in the springform pan.
  6. Bake for 55-60 minutes, or until the center is set and the top is lightly browned.
  7. Remove from the oven and let cool in the pan for about 10 minutes. Then, carefully remove the sides of the springform pan.
  8. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours, or overnight, before serving.
