What is an American Flag?

The American flag is a symbol of the United States of America. It consists of thirteen horizontal stripes, alternating red and white, representing the original thirteen colonies, and a blue rectangle in the top left corner with fifty white stars, representing the fifty states. The flag is often displayed on national holidays, military events, and other patriotic occasions. It is a symbol of pride and unity for many Americans.
