History and Myth of Independence Day 2022 Philippines

The history of Independence Day in the Philippines dates back to June 12, 1898, when the country declared its independence from Spanish colonial rule. This declaration of independence, known as the Proclamation of Philippine Independence, was read by General Emilio Aguinaldo in Kawit, Cavite.

However, despite this declaration, the country still faced challenges in achieving true independence. The Philippines went on to experience the American colonial period and World War II, before finally gaining full independence on July 4, 1946. In 1962, President Diosdado Macapagal moved the date of Independence Day from July 4 to June 12 to commemorate the original declaration of independence.

There are also myths and legends surrounding the events leading up to the declaration of independence. One of the most famous stories is the Cry of Pugad Lawin, where Andres Bonifacio and his fellow revolutionaries tore their cedulas (identification cards) as a symbol of their unity and determination to fight for independence.
