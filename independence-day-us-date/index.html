The history of Independence Day dates back to the American Revolution, which began in 1775. Despite its historical significance, there are also myths and misconceptions surrounding Independence Day. One common myth is that the Declaration of Independence was signed on July 4, 1776, by all the representatives of the thirteen colonies. In reality, the document was not signed by all the representatives on that day. The signing took place over a period of several months, with some representatives not signing until later in the year. Another myth is that the signing of the Declaration of Independence was a widely celebrated event at the time. It wasn't until the early 19th century that Independence Day became a widely celebrated national holiday.
