History and Myth

Wimbledon has a rich history that dates back to 1877. It is the oldest tennis tournament in the world and is held annually at the All England Lawn Tennis and Croquet Club in London. Over the years, Wimbledon has become synonymous with tradition, elegance, and sporting excellence. The tournament is known for its iconic grass courts, strict dress code, and the famous strawberries and cream served to spectators. Despite its long history, Wimbledon continues to captivate audiences around the world with its thrilling matches and legendary players.

For example, did you know that the grass at Wimbledon is cut to a precise height of 8 millimeters? Or that the tournament has its own hawk named Rufus, who is trained to scare away pigeons? These hidden secrets add to the charm and mystique of Wimbledon, making it a truly unique sporting event.
