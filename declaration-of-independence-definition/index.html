History and Myth of the Declaration of Independence Definition

The Declaration of Independence holds a special place in American history. It marks the beginning of the American Revolution and the birth of a new nation. The document itself was drafted by a committee of five men, including Thomas Jefferson, John Adams, and Benjamin Franklin. It was then presented to the Continental Congress, where it was debated and eventually adopted.

Over the years, the Declaration of Independence has taken on a mythical status. It is often portrayed as a heroic and noble act, with the Founding Fathers bravely standing up to British tyranny. While there is certainly truth to this narrative, it is important to remember that the declaration was also a political document. It was a means of justifying the colonies' decision to break away from British rule and gain international support for their cause.
